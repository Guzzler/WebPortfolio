## Responsive And Accessibility Goals
- Keep the timeline readable and balanced at desktop widths without making the center-aligned layout feel busy or fragile.
- Ensure any new arc labels or structural markers collapse cleanly on tablet and mobile without horizontal overflow or dense wrapping.
- Preserve semantic heading order so screen-reader users can understand the Experience narrative and the transition into Games.
- Avoid hover-only meaning; any visual grouping or emphasis should still be clear on touch devices.
- Maintain strong contrast and visible focus states in both dark and light mode for any new links, chips, or interactive controls.

## Risks To Avoid
- Turning the Experience section into generic personal-brand messaging instead of grounded career framing.
- Overexplaining the narrative so the timeline becomes harder, not easier, to skim.
- Inventing unsupported claims about impact, ownership, or motivation beyond what the current portfolio can honestly support.
- Creating too many badges or parallel labels that clutter the existing centered timeline layout.
- Breaking responsiveness, especially on narrow screens where the timeline already has to do more with less space.
- Losing the human and creative dimension by treating Games as a disconnected extra instead of a real part of Sharang's builder identity.
